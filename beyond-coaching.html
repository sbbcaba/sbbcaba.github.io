The Framework: ASK – TELL – MAKE

1
ASK (The Investigator)
Goal:
Eliminate Excuses.
"Is there any barrier I don't know about?"
Trigger:
Final verification they have the tools/knowledge to succeed.
2
TELL (The Director)
Goal:
Establish the Mandate.
"I am directing you to do X; failure to do so results in Y."
Trigger:
Removing all ambiguity. The employee must understand their job is at risk.
3
MAKE (The Executor)
Goal:
Enforce Accountability.
"Because X did not happen, we are proceeding with Y."
Trigger:
Protecting the business by following through on stated consequences.

Operational Speed: Why "Quickly" is a Metric

In complex discipline, slowness is a liability.

Team Erosion

High-performers lose faith when toxic behavior or incompetence lingers.

Legal Risk

Inconsistent timelines make discipline look arbitrary.

Resource Drain

Every hour spent "re-coaching" failure is stolen from your best assets.

The Goal: Clinical Efficiency. If the "Tell" phase fails, move to "Make" immediately.

Executing the "Tell" with Precision

Where cases get stuck: Ambiguous communication in the "Tell" phase.

The 3 Components of a Successful "Tell":

  • The Directive:
    A clear, non-negotiable instruction.
  • The Deadline:
    A specific date/time for compliance.
  • The Consequence:
    A transparent statement of what happens if missed.
EXAMPLE SCRIPT

"I am directing you to complete the safety audit by Friday at 4 PM. If this is not completed accurately and on time, we will move forward with a formal recommendation for termination."

"
